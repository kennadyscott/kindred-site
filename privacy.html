Privacy at Kindred

No personal data, by design.

You came here with things that are hard to say out loud. Protecting them isn't a policy for us — it's the product.

The short version: your check-ins, saved tools, explorations, and reflections stay on your device. Our analytics counts button presses, never people. And no one at Kindred — including administrators — can see your personal data, because our systems are built so it is never collected in the first place.

What stays on your device

  • Your check-in answers and how you said you're feeling
  • Tools and articles you save to My Kindred
  • What you type into exercises like Brain Dump — written, sorted, and gone
  • Which feelings, topics, and life situations you explore

None of this is transmitted to Kindred. Close the page and it's still yours alone.

What we count — anonymously

To make Kindred better, we count how often buttons are pressed: how many people chose "I'm looking for therapy," how many started a breathing tool, how many therapists reached checkout. These are plain totals with a strict allow-list of non-personal details (like which membership plan).

  • No names, emails, or profile text in analytics — ever
  • No user IDs, session IDs, or IP addresses stored
  • No tracking you across the web, no ad pixels, no data sales
  • No check-in answers or exercise content, in any form

Therapist accounts

Therapists who join Kindred provide professional profile information (name, credentials, license) that is, by its nature, public-facing — it's shown to clients. Billing runs on the web through our payment processor; Kindred staff dashboards show aggregate totals (signups, plan mix), not personal records.

This site is an early prototype. Before Kindred handles real accounts at scale, this page will grow into a full legal privacy policy — but the principle above is the constraint the product is being built around, not a promise added after.

Accessibility

Feeling understood includes being able to use the site. Kindred is built with:

  • Semantic headings, labeled controls, and ARIA roles for screen readers
  • Keyboard access for interactive flows, with visible focus states
  • Color contrast tuned against our cream backgrounds, and meaning never carried by color alone
  • Readable type at comfortable sizes, and layouts that reflow to any screen

We won't always get it right. If anything on Kindred is hard for you to use — a control you can't reach, text you can't read, motion that bothers you — tell us at hello@kindred.care and we'll fix it with care and priority.

One more thing

If you're in crisis, privacy shouldn't be the thing you're weighing. Call or text 988 — free, confidential, 24/7. You don't have to be suicidal to reach out.
